• High Technologies: Ukraine in the Hi-Tech Race

The position of Ukraine on the world market of high technologies is very low despite its high potential the country inherited after the collapse of the USSR. The export is largely raw material-related and is based on metallurgy and agriculture. At the same time, the world market of hi-tech products three times exceeds raw material industries.
Ukraine still has all the possibilities to create the basis for the development of high technologies in the country.
Can taxation privileges offered for the IT sector help keep the tempos of growth? When will real product companies appear in Ukraine, and at the expense of what can it happen? How does the creation of growth points such as technopolises and innovation parks influence the overall condition of the hi-tech sector? How efficient is the use of the developments of the military complex enterprises? Can the business offer large-scale changes in the sector and show real possibilities of development in the conditions when the state underestimates hi-tech industry? Which possibilities exist for the state to properly finance hi-tech industry development?

 

• Agriculture: Mining or Processing Industry?

Agriculture has a huge potential for development in Ukraine, due to fertile Ukrainian soil from one side and increasing food needs around the world – from another. The growth of grain production up to 100 million tons per year for the last 7 years is a real challenge.
However, the high volume growth does not guarantee the correlation in increase of employment and GDP growth. There is always a risk for the industry to turn into an inefficient sector like coal mining or metal mining.
How to ensure a high level of added value? How to attract high technologies to the sector?
What reasons prevent a “growth jump” in the sector?
What is the role of subsidies in increasing of industry efficiency?
What should be the role of the government in the development sector? Should the state regulate the export and import of agricultural seed?
What is the best (recommended) size of land for efficient agriculture farm? What should be the land market in Ukraine?
How to address employment issues in the conditions of developed agriculture?
What percentage of organic farming should be in the agriculture sector?
Is there any ground for concerns regarding soil exhaustion and environmental degradation in the light of new technologies use in agriculture?

 

• Machine Building: A Burden or Wings?

Engineering in any country is the key to the economy development. In Ukraine, this sector is represented by the companies that keep using technologies invented in the early 1990s. Today the shift to the de-industrialisation in Ukrainian economy is a reality.
However, globally, engineering is one of the key points for the development in post-industrial economy and the basis for “knowledge economy ”
Is it possible to upgrade old and outdated industrial enterprises to make them influential players in the global market?
Automotive production in Ukraine – myth or reality?
Does the Ukrainian economy need heavy machinery sector?
How productive is the idea for Ukraine to become a "factory of Europe", concentrating assembly plants with varying degrees of localisation?
How to replace the lost Russian market with other markets?
What could the engineering do for the development of engineering education and research?

• The Role of Leaders in the Development of Ukraine

The role of leaders is critical for the progress of any organisation. The absence of professional government managers in Ukraine hampers economic growth and leads to disastrous consequences.
Today we need to create of new class of professional government managers who can take responsibility for the future of Ukraine and lead the country to the prosperity.
Is it possible to completely replace all administrative staff in the government? Is it possible to train and renew the existing staff? What tools could be used for this?
How to ensure quality selection of the new leaders for the government management, not tainted by corruption?
Can the salary for the government employees be increased?
Is it possible to attract the professional managers of high level to work for government?
What institutions could be used for selection and training of the new government workers? Do we need to launch a special school to train future reformers?
What is the role of business in creating the new managerial elite – is it an observer or active participant?

 

• Financing of Development Projects

To finance the economy growth it is crucial to provide necessary conditions for an increase in domestic investment.
Ukraine has not yet established a financial infrastructure that would meet the needs of the economy.
What urgent steps should be done to renew lending system to medium-sized businesses?
Do we need to save banking sector and how to encourage it for reduction of interest on loans?
What can the European Bank of Reconstruction and Development do for Ukraine’s economy?
Can the creation of private pension funds contribute to the creation of long-term resources of cheap financing and how to teach people to be investors?
What are the prospects of the stock market in Ukraine?
Ukraine is the only European country that did not create export-credit agency. When the state will help Ukrainian exporters in their expansion into foreign markets, securing insurance risks, providing funding and government guarantees?
Is money emission a panacea or fiction? Can the state be a catalyst for foreign and domestic private investment? Is it possible to create an effective public financing in priority sectors of economy? How to prevent corruption?
What is the role of loans from international financial institutions in building capacity for early stage of real economy financing?
Do you need to finance consumption growth?
State leasing company and the State Mortgage Institution – do we really need them?

 

• Educational Revolution for the Country and its Business

Combining Education and Science, with further use of scientific achievements for business do have a legislative basis. Positive shift here is possible in case the legislative changes will be backed up with funding and the establishment of appropriate structures and institutions.
This will result into the growth of business and GDP growth after 3-10 years of development.
How to convince the society that a change in priorities and concentration of resources and efforts on this issue is absolutely crucial for education?
Online education – how to make it not a “fashionable trend”, but an effective tool for preparing the population to meet contemporary development challenges.
How could Ukraine ensure the transition to the economy of creativity, given that we still have not succeeded in the creation of “knowledge economy”?
Who will be our children? What skills do they need in the 21 century?

• Growth Points: A Winning Strategy in the Global Competition

"Points of growth" – is it a winning strategy in a global competition or the source of corruption?
For rapid economic growth the country's leadership choice varies between two poles – the so called unregulated “open market” and the creation of "growth points" – the industries and areas with special conditions for business regulation.
In Ukrainian realities well known global tools of local development often became a source of corruption. That is why the use of special tools for quick and powerful start of the economy became a topic for political and economy discussion in society, despite the strong support of business.
Does the creation of "growth points" significantly increase the GDP growth rate?

• Public Private Partnerships. Reloaded

There are three models of the interaction of business and government. The first is when the government totally manages the economy. The second is when the government does not influence the economy at all and acts only as a “night watchman”. The third is when the government controls adherence to the macroeconomic indicators, and also designs development strategies together with business and provides for the necessary conditions, while also acting as the promoter of business on the international markets.
Ukraine has tried the first two models that turned out to be inefficient. Business and government have to find new rules of interaction.
What is necessary for the efficient partnership of business and government?
What is role of industrial business associations in the process of such interaction?
Can business consolidate its position in case of the creation of a centralised association of business, something like the Austrian Chamber of Commerce?
Which state bodies can efficiently work in order to provide the existence of market economy and yet have adequate possibilities to monitor, forecast and plan?
When will the triangle of business, government and society work in Ukraine?
Who has to play the role of business ombudsman?
